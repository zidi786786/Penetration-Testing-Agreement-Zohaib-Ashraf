---

## **1. Introduction**
This Penetration Testing Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into between:

- **Pentester:** *Zohaib Ashraf*  
- **Client:** *ParoCyber*

This agreement defines the authorized scope, rules, rights, and responsibilities related to security testing on Client-approved systems.

---

## **2. Purpose of Engagement**
The purpose of this engagement is to identify vulnerabilities, assess cyber risks, and strengthen the overall security posture of the Client’s digital assets. All findings remain confidential.

---

## **3. Scope of Testing**

### **3.1 In-Scope (Authorized Assets)**  
#### **Web Application**  
- `https://portal.parocyber.com`  
**Objective:** Identify exploitable web vulnerabilities.

#### **Network Range**  
- `192.168.10.50 – 192.168.10.200`  
**Objective:** Detect misconfigurations and exposed services.

#### **Mobile Application**  
- *ParoCyber Android App v3.0*  
**Objective:** Evaluate API security, storage controls, and permissions.

#### **APIs**  
- `/v1/auth`  
- `/v1/data`  
**Objective:** Test access control, token security, and injection prevention.

---

### **3.2 Out-of-Scope (Unless Explicitly Approved)**
- DoS/DDoS  
- Physical attacks  
- Social engineering  
- Malware deployment  
- Data deletion, corruption, or manipulation  
- Unapproved networks, IPs, or applications  

---

## **4. Rules of Engagement (ROE)**
- Testing will occur only during the approved window.  
- Sensitive data must not be stored or copied.  
- No destructive activities are allowed.  
- No brute-force attacks unless explicitly permitted.  
- Pentester must stop testing if system instability is observed.  
- Client emergency contact must be notified for critical vulnerabilities.

---

## **5. Testing Methodology**

### **Standards & Frameworks**
- **OWASP Web Security Testing Guide**  
- **PTES** (Penetration Testing Execution Standard)  
- **NIST SP 800-115**  
- **MITRE ATT&CK**

### **Tools**
Nmap, Burp Suite, Nessus/OpenVAS, Wireshark, Gobuster, Maltego, OSINT tools, and custom scripts.

---

## **6. Client Responsibilities**
- Provide accurate system information  
- Issue official written authorization  
- Assign an emergency technical contact  
- Inform internal teams about testing schedule  
- Maintain up-to-date system backups  
- Provide necessary credentials (VPN, test accounts)  
- Approve final scope and any high-risk testing  
- Acknowledge inherent risks associated with security testing  

---

## **7. Pentester Responsibilities**
- Follow scope and ROE strictly  
- Use safe, non-destructive testing techniques  
- Maintain complete confidentiality  
- Stop testing immediately upon critical findings  
- Securely delete all sensitive data after project completion  
- Provide clear and professional documentation  
- Use only authorized devices and IPs  
- Comply with local and international cybersecurity laws  

---

## **8. Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)**
All accessed or discovered information is confidential.  
Applicable laws include:

- Pakistan **PECA 2016**  
- **GDPR**  
- **CCPA**  
- Other applicable international regulations  

Unauthorized disclosure may result in legal penalties.

---

## **9. Legal & Compliance Liability**
- Pentester is protected from liability when operating within the approved scope.  
- Client must ensure authorization letters are valid.  
- Pentester is not liable for existing system vulnerabilities or unintentional disruptions unless caused by negligence or misuse.
